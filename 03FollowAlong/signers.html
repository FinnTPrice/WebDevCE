Who Were the Signers?

Famous Signers

The Declaration of Independence was signed by 56 delegates to the Continental Congress. Some of the most famous signers include:

John Hancock
Known for his bold signature, Hancock was the President of the Continental Congress at the time. His name became synonymous with "signature" due to how prominent his autograph was on the document.
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration, Jefferson went on to become the third President of the United States and is a major figure in American history.
Benjamin Franklin
A founding father, scientist, and diplomat, Franklin was one of the most respected figures in America and abroad. He later served as the U.S. Ambassador to France and played a crucial role in securing French support during the American Revolution.
John Adams
A key advocate for independence, Adams was a leader in the Continental Congress and would later become the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, Samuel was a firebrand for independence and a leader of the Massachusetts rebels who pushed for resistance to British rule.
Richard Henry Lee
Known for proposing the resolution for independence at the Continental Congress, Lee was a strong advocate for the break from Britain.
Charles Carroll
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration, Carroll was a wealthy landowner and a strong supporter of independence. He later served in the U.S. Senate.
Francis Lewis
A wealthy merchant and public official, Lewis was an important figure in the Revolutionary War effort.
Elbridge Gerry
A delegate from Massachusetts, Gerry later became the fifth Vice President of the United States.
Roger Sherman
A representative from Connecticut, Sherman is the only person to have signed all four major founding documents: the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
