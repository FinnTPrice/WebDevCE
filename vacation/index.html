Budget Vacations

Don't Overspend for Memories

Founded in 1998, Budget Vacations began as a tiny, two-person operation run out of a converted garage in Clearwater, Florida. Its founders, best friends Mia Torres and Daniel Blake, believed that travel shouldn't be a luxury reserved for the wealthy. Their early business model was simple: negotiate discounted group rates with small hotels and bus operators, then bundle them into ultra-affordable vacation packages.

By 2005, Budget Vacations had expanded across the Southeast, offering $199 weekend beach trips that became wildly popular among college students and young families.
